Armenia

Armenia is located in the South Caucasus region of Eurasia, bordered by Georgia to the north, Azerbaijan to the east, Iran to the south, and Turkey to the west. Its capital and largest city is Yerevan. Armenia has a rich history and cultural heritage, with evidence of human settlement in the region dating back to the Neolithic era. The Kingdom of Armenia was established in the 6th century BCE, and at its height, it spanned from the Mediterranean Sea to the Caspian Sea. Over the centuries, Armenia was invaded and ruled by various empires, including the Roman, Byzantine, Arab, Persian, and Ottoman empires. Armenia became part of the Soviet Union in 1922 and gained independence in 1991 following the collapse of the Soviet Union. Today, Armenia is a parliamentary republic with a population of approximately 3 million people. The country is known for its ancient monasteries, stunning landscapes, and delicious cuisine, including dishes like lavash (flatbread) and dolma (stuffed grape leaves).

The Kingdom of Armenia was the first nation to adopt Christianity as its official religion in 301 AD. That legacy lives on in the 4,000-plus Armenian Apostolic churches and monasteries throughout the country.

Armenia is world-renowned for its unique ecclesiastical architecture. Because Christianity has been around for so long, it has entirely naturally become interwoven with Armenian identity and influences almost every aspect of local culture, especially architecture.

Lake Sevan

url

Google Earth

Lake Sevan is a large freshwater lake in Armenia, located in the eastern part of the country. It is the largest lake in Armenia and one of the largest high-altitude lakes in the world, with a surface area of approximately 1,242 square kilometres and a maximum depth of about 80 meters.

Lake Sevan is an essential natural resource for Armenia, providing drinking water, hydroelectric power, and a habitat for various plant and animal species. The lake is also a popular tourist destination, with many visitors coming to enjoy its natural beauty.

Yerevan

url

YouTube

Yerevan is the capital and largest city of Armenia. It has a population of approximately 1 million people and is one of the world’s oldest continuously inhabited cities, with a history dating back to the 8th century BC.

Yerevan is known for its unique blend of ancient and modern architecture, with notable landmarks including the Republic Square, the Cascade complex, and the Matenadaran Institute of Ancient Manuscripts. The city is also home to numerous museums, theatres, art galleries, and a vibrant food and nightlife scene.

Dilijan National Park

url

Armenian Geographic

The park is known for its rich biodiversity, with over 1000 plant species and various animal species, including brown bears, wolves, lynxes, and birds. It is also home to numerous cultural and historical landmarks, such as the Haghartsin Monastery and the Goshavank Monastery.

Visitors to Dilijan National Park can enjoy various outdoor activities, such as hiking, camping, and wildlife watching. There are also several trails and hiking routes that visitors can follow, with varying levels of difficulty.
